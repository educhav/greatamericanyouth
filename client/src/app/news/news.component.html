The incident has sparked controversy and debate about the use of condoms and personal freedom. Some people are calling the man a hero for standing up for his beliefs, while others are condemning his actions as dangerous and irresponsible.

The man's protest comes at a time when there is a growing concern about sexually transmitted infections (STIs) in the United States. The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) reports that there are approximately 20 million new cases of STIs in the country each year.

Governor Tony Evers has weighed in on the incident, in a WKOW interview at his residence, expressing outrage that the man was arrested. Evers said, "While I cannot condone his assault on a police officer, I am deeply troubled by the arrest of this man. The right to free speech and peaceful protest is a fundamental cornerstone of our democracy, and we must protect it at all costs."

Evers concluded his remark by agreeing with Patt on the matter of condoms being a "rubber prison" and heatedly expressed that condoms were merely a "non-factual construct fabricated by academic snobs who have never gotten their dick sucked in a Motel6 while shooting darts to arbitrarily decide which districts in Wisconsin get sufficient public school funding."

Evers concluded his statements by apologizing for his "primal" outburst and retreated inside of his 100 square foot shed, where he continues his search for solving the homeless crisis in Madison.

The governor's response highlights the tension between public health and personal freedom. It remains to be seen whether the incident will lead to any changes in public policy or attitudes towards condom use and cannabis legalization.

For now, the man's actions have sparked a lively debate and drawn attention to an important issue. Whether or not he will be remembered as a hero or a villain remains to be seen.
